Critical Business Insights - RFM Analysis

Key Findings

The Hidden Problem in the Data

When analyzing 96,478 customers, I discovered that 40% of the base is in a critical situation (At Risk + Lost). This represents a risk of $1.26M in future revenue.

The Opportunity

The "Champions" and "Loyal Customers" segments have high average ticket values ($32.79 and $31.50), but represent only 36% of the base. Retention strategies could increase revenue by up to 28%.

Critical Business Insights

1. RED ALERT: "At Risk" Customers are the biggest concern

  • 23.9% of the base (23,102 customers) are at risk of churn
  • Represent ~$740K in monetary value (the highest among all segments)
  • Action needed: Urgent reactivation campaigns (discounts, personalized emails, recovery)

2. Solid base of loyal customers, but underutilized

  • Loyal Customers (19.9% - 19,187 customers) generate ~$604K
  • Champions (16.2% - 15,597 customers) generate ~$512K
  • Opportunity: These 36% of the base are responsible for nearly half the revenue. Loyalty programs/upselling can maximize this group

3. "Lost" Customers still have recovery potential

  • 16.1% (15,490 customers) already lost, but generated ~$498K in the past
  • Strategy: Aggressive win-back campaigns with strong incentives

4. "Promising" segment needs nurturing

  • 15.9% (15,312 customers) are new buyers with potential
  • Average value per customer: $32.33 (second best!)
  • Action: Onboarding journey to transform them into Champions

5. Risk concentration

  • 40% of the base (At Risk + Lost) represents customers in critical situation
  • If no action is taken, the company could lose ~$1.26M in LTV

6. Disparity in value per customer

  • Champions: $32.79/customer (best monetization)
  • Promising: $32.33/customer (second best)
  • Lost: $32.15/customer
  • At Risk: $32.06/customer (high value, but inactive)
  • Loyal Customers: $31.50/customer
  • Others: $30.12/customer
  • Insight: High-value customers are concentrated in at-risk segments

Data-Driven Strategic Recommendations

HIGH Priority (Immediate)

1. Reactivate "At Risk":

  • 20% discount coupon with limited time
  • Personalized email: "We miss you!"
  • Push notifications in app

2. Recover "Lost":

  • Satisfaction survey: "Why did you stop buying?"
  • Aggressive return offer (free shipping + discount)

MEDIUM Priority (30-60 days)

3. Strengthen "Loyal Customers":

  • Points/cashback program
  • Early access to new products
  • Exclusive benefits

4. Accelerate "Promising":

  • Second purchase with welcome discount
  • Personalized recommendations via email marketing

LOW Priority (Long term)

5. Optimize "Others":

  • A/B test campaigns to understand behavior
  • More granular segmentation

Potential Financial Impact

If we successfully execute the above strategies:

  • Reactivate 20% of "At Risk" → +$148K in revenue
  • Recover 10% of "Lost" → +$50K in revenue
  • Convert 30% of "Promising" to Champions → +$148K in additional LTV

Total recovery potential: ~$346K

Questions to Deepen the Analysis

During the analysis, I discovered that the dataset contains data that enables further valid questions. My analysis doesn't cover these, but they're worth investigating. I'll pursue these in a more robust project than this one.

  • Which acquisition channel brings more "At Risk"? (Social media? Google Ads?)
  • Is there seasonality in churn? (Do customers abandon in specific months?)
  • Product categories associated with each segment? (What categories do At Risk buy more from?)
  • Average time until churn? (How many days after the last purchase does a customer become "At Risk"?)
  • Performance by state/region? (Are there states with more churn?)
  • Impact of payment method? (Do customers who use installments have higher retention?)
